Shepherd’s Pie Gnocchi

Prep Time: 10 minutes    Cook Time: 25 minutes    Total Time: 35 minutes    Yield: 4


Ingredients

  • 1 pound gnocchi
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
  • 1/2 yellow onion, finely diced
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 3 sprigs fresh thyme, leaves removed from stems
  • Pinch of fresh rosemary leaves, finely chopped
  • 1 pound ground lamb (or ground beef)
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 3/4 teaspoon salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 3/4 cup beef broth
  • 1/2 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 1 1/4 cups peas and carrots
  • 1/4 cup heavy cream
  • 1/3 cup finely grated parmesan
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons finely chopped parsley

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a boil. Add the gnocchi and cook until they float to the top. Once they float, they’re done. Drain and set aside. You can also cook them once the sauce is done since it's quick.

  2. Heat the olive oil and butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced onion and cook until softened and golden around the edges. Stir in the garlic and cook until fragrant.

  3. Add the tomato paste, thyme, and rosemary. Cook for another two minutes, letting the tomato paste caramelize slightly.

  4. Add the ground lamb and cook, breaking it up, until browned and cooked through. Season with smoked paprika, red pepper flakes, salt, and black pepper.

  5. Pour in the Worcestershire sauce to deglaze the pan, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom for extra flavor.

  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the beef broth and cornstarch, then pour it into the skillet. Simmer until the sauce thickens slightly.

  7. Add the peas and carrots and cook until tender.

  8. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream, parmesan, and chopped parsley until everything is creamy and well combined.

  9. Add the cooked gnocchi and gently toss until fully coated in the sauce.

Lastly, serve and enjoy. Back to Top


Chicken Alfredo Lasagna Soup

Prep Time: 15 minutes    Cook Time: 30 minutes    Total Time: 45 minutes    Yield: 4


Ingrediants

Chicken:

  • 1 pound chicken breast (about 2 large breasts)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus more for pan
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • 1 teaspoon lemon pepper
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ¼ teaspoon black pepper

Soup:

  • 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 tablespoon minced garlic
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1½ cups half and half or heavy cream
  • 1½ cups whole milk
  • 1½ teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • ½ teaspoon dried oregano
  • ½ teaspoon salt, adjust to taste
  • ½ teaspoon black pepper
  • Dash of nutmeg
  • Dash of dried mustard
  • Dash of cayenne, to taste
  • 8 to 9 ounces lasagna sheets, broken into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons parsley, finely chopped
  • ¼ to ⅓ cup freshly grated parmesan
  • 2 tablespoons basil, chopped
  • ⅓ cup shredded mozzarella, more to taste
  • 1 to 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour (optional, for thickening)

Instructions

Marinate and Cook Chicken:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, Italian seasoning, lemon pepper, smoked paprika, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper.

  2. Add chicken breasts to a large bowl, pour the marinade over them, and toss until evenly coated.

  3. Heat a drizzle of oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.

  4. Sear the chicken for 5 to 6 minutes per side, until golden and fully cooked through.

  5. Transfer to a plate and let it cool slightly, then chop into bite-sized chunks. Set aside until ready to add later.

Make the Soup Base:

  1. In the same pot over medium heat, melt the butter and sauté minced garlic for 1 minute, until fragrant and lightly golden.

  2. Pour in chicken broth, cream or half and half, and milk, stirring to combine.

  3. Add Italian seasoning, oregano, salt, black pepper, nutmeg, dried mustard, and cayenne.

  4. Bring to a gentle simmer over low-medium heat to let the flavors meld together.

Cook the Pasta:

  1. Break lasagna sheets into smaller pieces and add them directly to the simmering soup.

  2. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking and cook for 12 to 14 minutes, or until the noodles are tender and silky.

Finishing Touches:

  1. Add the chopped chicken back into the pot along with parmesan, mozzarella, parsley, and basil.

  2. Stir until the cheeses melt and the soup turns creamy.

  3. Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

(Optional) Thicken the Soup:

  1. If you prefer a thicker consistency, ladle out 1 cup of the soup into a separate bowl.

  2. Whisk in flour until smooth, then pour the mixture back into the pot.

  3. Stir and let it simmer briefly until the soup slightly thickens.

Lastly, serve and enjoy. Back to Top


Jalapeno Cheddar Tuna Melt Crunchwrap

Prep Time: 20 minutes    Cook Time: 15 minutes    Total Time: 35 minutes    Yield: 4


Ingredients

Tuna Salad Filling

  • 1 (12-ounce) can solid white albacore tuna, drained
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/3 cup red onions, chopped small
  • 1/3 cup sweet yellow corn
  • 1 jalapeño, seeds and stem removed, finely chopped
  • 1/2 tablespoon yellow mustard
  • 1 tablespoon chopped parsley
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, add to taste
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • squeeze of lemon juice

For Assembly

  • 5 large flour tortillas (burrito size) — cut 1 into 4 smaller circles for sealing
  • 1 head romaine lettuce, thinly chopped
  • 1 (8-ounce) block sharp cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 1 bag nacho-flavored tortilla chips
  • 1/4 cup pickled jalapeños (tamed or mild)
  • neutral oil, for toasting

Instructions

Make the tuna salad filling:

  1. In a mixing bowl, combine the drained tuna, mayonnaise, red onions, corn, jalapeño, yellow mustard, chopped parsley, juice of half a lemon, salt, and pepper. Mix until creamy and well combined.

Prep the sealing tortillas:

  1. Take one large flour tortilla and cut it into four smaller circles using a bowl or lid as a guide. Set aside — these are for sealing the Crunchwraps.

Assemble the Crunchwraps:

  1. Lay a large tortilla flat on your work surface. Add a layer of shredded cheddar cheese right in the center.

  2. Spoon some of the tuna salad mixture over the cheese, keeping it in a tight, circular mound.

  3. Arrange whole triangular nacho-flavored tortilla chips over the tuna, overlapping slightly as needed to form a full circle that covers the filling.

  4. Add a layer of chopped romaine lettuce on top of the chips, followed by a few pickled jalapeño slices.

  5. Sprinkle more shredded cheddar cheese over everything.

  6. Place one of the small tortilla circles on top to cover the filling.

  7. Heat a pan over medium heat and drizzle in a little neutral oil. Place the Crunchwrap seam-side down in the pan and toast for 2 to 3 minutes until golden brown and sealed.

  8. Flip and cook the other side for another 2 to 3 minutes until the tortilla is crisp and the cheese inside is melted.

  9. Slice and serve while hot — melty, cheesy, spicy, and packed with crunch!

Lastly, serve and enjoy. Back to Top
